# Acme — Client Context

This file loads automatically whenever I work inside Acme's folder, so every
specialist starts knowing this client. It is the single source of truth for
Acme. Keep it current. End every task by folding in what changed. A stale
context misleads the whole team.

## Snapshot
- What they do: B2B scheduling software for home-services teams (plumbers,
  electricians, HVAC).
- Stage: Series A, ~40 people, growing fast.
- My engagement: fractional growth lead, 2 days/week, since Jan 2026.
- Main contact: Dana Reyes, Head of Marketing. Decision-maker: CEO, Sam Cole.

## Goals (this quarter)
- Primary: lift trial-to-paid conversion from 18% to 25%.
- Secondary: build a repeatable content engine (4 posts/month).
- Not a goal right now: paid acquisition. We're fixing conversion first.

## Audience
- Buyer: owner or ops lead of a 5-50 person home-services business.
- Not technical. Time-poor. Skeptical of "software that promises everything."
- Cares about: getting paid faster, fewer missed jobs, less phone tag.
- Reads: trade forums, Facebook groups, YouTube how-tos. Not LinkedIn.

## Voice and tone
- Plain, practical, no-nonsense. Talk like a fellow operator, not a vendor.
- Confident but never hypey. Show, don't boast.
- Short sentences. Real examples over abstractions.

## Banned words and rules
- No em dashes.
- Banned: revolutionary, seamless, game-changing, leverage (as a verb),
  "in today's fast-paced world."
- Never imply features we don't have. Check with Dana if unsure.
- Always say "trial," never "free trial" (legal preference).

## Constraints
- Reviews and approvals go through Dana, then a 24h legal check for claims.
- Don't reference competitors by name in public-facing copy.

## Useful context
- Their edge: setup in under an hour, real human support. Lean on these.
- Their weak spot: mobile app is still rough. Don't over-promise it.
